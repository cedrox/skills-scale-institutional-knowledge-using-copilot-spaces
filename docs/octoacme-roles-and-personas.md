# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates team-level agile practices, removes impediments, and coaches the team on effective delivery habits.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, and demos
- Remove or escalate impediments blocking the team
- Coach the team on agile practices and team dynamics
- Help ensure backlog items are ready for development
- Track and help improve team flow and cycle time

### Goals
- Keep the team focused and unblocked
- Improve predictability and team delivery health
- Foster continual improvement and psychological safety

### Typical Communication / Interactions
- Works closely with Project Manager (PM) for scheduling and risk visibility
- Collaborates with Product Manager (PdM) to ensure stories are ready and understood
- Supports Developers and QA in removing impediments
- Flags escalations to PM when cross-team or organizational support is needed

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
The UX Designer focuses on user research, interaction design, and delivering validated, usable interfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design interaction flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Provide design specifications and acceptance criteria
- Collaborate on accessibility and usability improvements
- Validate designs against success metrics post-release

### Goals
- Deliver usable, accessible interfaces that meet user needs
- Reduce rework from usability issues
- Increase product adoption and satisfaction

### Typical Communication / Interactions
- Partners with PdM to define user problems and success criteria
- Works with Developers and QA to ensure design intent is implemented and testable
- Engages with Project Manager for timelines and design handoffs

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
The Technical Lead provides technical direction, ensures architectural consistency, and mentors the engineering team.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate technical architecture and standards
- Lead design and code reviews for complex areas
- Make trade-off decisions for performance, scalability, and maintainability
- Identify technical risks and mitigation plans
- Support estimation for complex work

### Goals
- Ensure long-term code quality and system reliability
- Reduce technical debt and avoid rework
- Enable the team to deliver sustainable solutions

### Typical Communication / Interactions
- Collaborates with Developers on implementation approaches
- Aligns with PM and PdM on technical feasibility and impact to schedule
- Coordinates with DevOps Engineer and Release Engineer on deployment constraints and infrastructure needs

---

## Release Engineer

### Role Summary
Release Engineer owns release pipelines, automation, and the integrity of deployments from staging to production.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and improve CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage release orchestration, tagging, and release notes
- Validate pipelines and pre-release checks (tests, smoke, security scans)
- Coordinate rollbacks and mitigation during problematic releases
- Ensure releases are reproducible and auditable

### Goals
- Reduce release risk and time-to-release
- Increase repeatability and observability of deployments
- Minimize production incidents related to deployment

### Typical Communication / Interactions
- Works with Developers, QA, and DevOps Engineer on pipeline and release validation
- Coordinates with PM for release windows and stakeholder notifications
- Handoffs to Customer Support Lead for support readiness and runbooks

---

## Customer Support Lead

### Role Summary
Customer Support Lead represents support and operations perspectives, ensuring readiness to handle customer-facing issues.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate support readiness for releases (runbooks, FAQs)
- Triage and escalate customer-reported issues
- Capture customer feedback and route to PdM or PM for prioritization
- Maintain support playbooks and contact lists

### Goals
- Reduce time to resolve customer issues
- Improve quality of support handoffs and incident communications
- Capture actionable feedback to improve product experience

### Typical Communication / Interactions
- Works with PM and PdM to prioritize customer-impacting fixes
- Coordinates with DevOps Engineer and Release Engineer for production diagnostics and rollback
- Communicates status to stakeholders and users during incidents

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage infrastructure, monitoring, and platform reliability to support delivery and operations.

### Responsibilities
- Provision and maintain infrastructure and environments
- Implement monitoring, alerting, and logging best practices
- Support CI/CD tooling and performance optimizations
- Assist with incident response and post-incident reviews
- Work on security hardening and compliance where applicable

### Goals
- Ensure reliable and scalable infrastructure
- Reduce toil and manual configuration through automation
- Improve observability and incident detection

### Typical Communication / Interactions
- Collaborates with Release Engineer for deployment pipelines and rollout strategies
- Works with Technical Lead and Developers on infrastructure needs and constraints
- Coordinates with Customer Support Lead for production issues and on-call rotations
